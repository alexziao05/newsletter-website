Talk it Out

Feeling stressed? Just need to talk with someone? Counselors from C&PS provide confidential support to SDSU students on a drop-in basis through the Talk It Out program.

Meet one-on-one with a counselor for 15-20 minutes to get perspective, ask questions, and learn about strategies and resources to address your concerns. Unlike psychotherapy, Talk It Out allows you to speak with a counselor informally to get their professional guidance without an appointment, much like visiting a professor's office hours.

28th annual Family Weekend

Family Weekend is in the fall semester and celebrates SDSU students, families and friends! It invites parents and family supporters to get a glimpse into their student’s life at SDSU. It is one of SDSU’s most long-standing traditions and brings thousands of students, parents and family supporters and alumni together. Several events are hosted throughout the weekend specifically for attendees. The signature events have included a picnic, brunch and a football tailgate.

Student Success Resources

All SDSU students are encouraged to download SDSU Safe, a free phone-based app to promote personal safety both on and off campus. The app contains information about ways to easily report crime tips and information about incidents, and you can use the app to have a friend track your movement between locations. More information, and links to download for Apple and Android devices, are available online.

October is Advising Month at SDSU

Get ready for Spring 2026 registration by making the most of Advising Month!

Throughout October, SDSU colleges and departments will be offering advising sessions, events, and workshops designed to help you plan your courses, stay on track with graduation requirements, and register for next semester.

Registration opens in early November, so now’s the time to connect with your advisor, ask questions, and create a plan for Spring!

Avoid the last-minute stress by taking charge of your academic path during Advising Month!
